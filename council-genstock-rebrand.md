# Council opinions: genstock.photo -> unstatic.art rebrand

## Claude

**Don't rebrand. Keep genstock.photo.**

Here's why: You're about to make a classic product-building mistake—letting feature expansion dictate naming before you know what actually resonates in market.

**The case against unstatic.art:**

1. **It's clever-obscure, not clear-memorable.** "Unstatic" requires explanation. When someone asks "what's that site you used?", your users will stumble over it. "Gen-stock" is immediately parseable even if narrow. I can *hear* the friction: "unstatic? You-in-static? How do you spell that?"

2. **The .art TLD is a trust tax.** Despite your sophisticated feature set, .art reads "side project" or "experimental" to many users. Stock photo buyers and illustration clients—your actual customers—are conservative. They trust .com, tolerate .io, question everything else.

3. **"Unstatic" undersells AND confuses.** It focuses on the technical mechanic (making static things dynamic) rather than the value outcome (coherent, reusable visual identities). Meanwhile "art" overshoots into fine-art territory that isn't your wheelhouse. You're not competing with DeviantArt or ArtStation.

4. **SEO disaster.** Nobody searches "unstatic" anything. "Genstock" has clear search intent signals around stock imagery and generation. You can still rank for "AI illustration," "character generation," etc. while anchored to that base.

**The case FOR genstock.photo (for now):**

Your identity-lock mechanism IS your moat, but you haven't proven which use case will drive growth. Is it book illustrators? Brand consistency? Character designers? Let the market tell you. "Genstock" is narrow enough to get initial traction while being generic enough to stretch.

**My actual recommendation:**

Launch as genstock.photo. If in 6-12 months the "stock photo" positioning is actively costing you deals or confusing your best users, rebrand then—with real data about what name would work. Right now you're guessing.

If you absolutely must change, **fix the domain first**: get unstatic.com or find something better entirely. Never launch a serious B2B-adjacent product on .art.

## Gemini

Alright, let's cut straight to it.

**`genstock.photo` is a problem. You absolutely need to rebrand.**

Your product delivers "book-illustration-quality output, era-remixed character reimaginings, and coherent multi-frame character walk cycles." It's an "identity-locked generative image platform" with a "Story" feature. `genstock.photo` actively sabotages this. "Stock photo" implies generic, cheap, mass-produced, and devoid of artistic value – the exact opposite of what you're achieving. It undersells your innovation so severely that it will deter your most valuable potential users (artists, illustrators, storytellers) who would immediately dismiss it as a commoditized tool. The `.photo` TLD just reinforces this limited, low-ambition perception.

**Now, regarding `unstatic.art`:**

It's a step in the right direction, a significant improvement over `genstock.photo`.
*   **What it gets right:** "art" broadens your scope appropriately and positions you within a creative context. The `.art` TLD is excellent for your target audience, signaling creativity and trust in that space. "unstatic" hints at dynamism and variation, which is part of your core mechanic.
*   **What it gets wrong:** "unstatic" is still a bit abstract and doesn't immediately convey your most powerful differentiator: the *identity-lock* and *cohesion*. It doesn't quite scream "consistent character generation across variations." It requires a mental leap from the user. For memorability and initial clarity, a name that more directly references character, identity, or narrative cohesion would be stronger. Its abstract nature also makes SEO a challenge; you'll have to heavily invest in brand recognition for "unstatic" to be a search term.

**My Opinion:** `unstatic.art` is **better, but not perfect**. It's a safe choice that doesn't actively harm your brand, but it doesn't fully capitalize on your unique selling proposition (USP).

**Rebrand Timing:** **NOW.** You're in soft-launch with friends and family. This is the perfect, low-cost window to correct course before wider public exposure (Product Hunt) and before you build any meaningful brand equity around the wrong name. Don't waste a single marketing dollar on `genstock.photo`.

**Alternative Name Worth Considering:**

Given your strength in "identity-lock," "character reimaginings," and "coherent multi-frame," I'd lean into that. Something like:

*   **`PersonaForge.art`**: This name highlights the creation ("Forge") of consistent characters ("Persona"). It's descriptive, memorable, implies power and craft, and pairs beautifully with the `.art` TLD. It captures the essence of generating variations while maintaining a core identity.

In summary: Kill `genstock.photo` immediately. `unstatic.art` is acceptable, but `PersonaForge.art` (or a similar construction emphasizing character/identity and creation) could be significantly stronger for clarity and impact, especially for your specialized offering.

## ChatGPT

First, let's critique "genstock.photo." It was initially descriptive but limited in portraying what you've built. The term "stock" pigeonholes the product into a conventional stock photo space, which doesn't capture the innovation and broader utility your platform now offers. "Photo" is similarly restrictive, especially since you're dealing with more than static imagery.

Now, onto "unstatic.art." This name immediately signals a break from convention, hinting at dynamism (“unstatic”) and creativity (“art”). It aligns better with the core functionality of your platform—transforming static images into dynamic, varied artistic expressions. It emphasizes movement and creativity, a strong differentiator from mere stock imagery.

**Pros of "unstatic.art":**
1. **Memorability & Originality**: The term "unstatic" is unusual and engaging. It suggests movement and change, which is consistent with what users would experience.
2. **Breadth**: Using ".art" instead of ".photo" broadens your market perception. It accommodates your product's artistic and innovative dimensions beyond photos.
3. **Differentiation**: It clearly distances you from traditional stock photo services, highlighting your unique features like the identity-lock mechanism.

**Cons of "unstatic.art":**
1. **SEO Challenges**: Neither "unstatic" nor ".art" will benefit SEO against more established terms. It may require extra marketing efforts to gain visibility.
2. **Domain Trust**: The ".art" domain is less common. While trendy, it might not carry the immediate trust of a ".com," possibly impacting click-through rates or perceived legitimacy.
3. **Clarity of Concept**: "Unstatic" could require explanation, especially for users unfamiliar with its play on motion versus static.

**Recommendation:** Rebrand now. You're at an ideal stage pre-Product Hunt to pivot and position yourself in a way that matches your product's evolved capabilities. However, ensure explanatory messaging accompanies the name. Consider complimentary messaging strategies, like showcasing demonstrations that vividly exemplify "unstatic" actions.

**Alternative Name:** If you're open to alternatives, consider "MorphCanvas.art" or "BeyondStill.art" for clearer indications of transformation while retaining artistic latitude. 

Ultimately, “unstatic.art” captures an intriguing tension between movement and art and represents your product's capabilities more accurately than "genstock.photo."

## Radium

**Unstatic.art is the better name, but only if you’re willing to own its trade-offs. I’d rebrand now.**

**What “unstatic” gets right:** It names the *mechanic*, not the *vertical*. Identity-lock + variation is exactly “un-static-ing” one image into a multiplicities. It’s distinctive, suggest motion without being frantic, and carves a unique semantic slot. “.art” TLD is earned here—you’re producing illustration-quality output, not mere photos. The domain is short, memorable when spoken, and visually clean.

**What it gets wrong:** “Unstatic” is an invented negation. It will be misread, mis-spelled, or forgotten as “Unst**a**tic” vs. “Unst**e**tic.” It says nothing about character lock, comparison, or the word-tag UX. SEO is near zero—no keyword commercial intent; you’ll have to build brand search from scratch. .art TLD still signals “portfolio/gallery” to institutional buyers (a risk if B2B licensing is a future revenue line). And “unstatic” sounds abstract-art collective, not a utility tool—you may attract the wrong early adopters (prompt dabblers vs. professional production users).

**Genstock.photo’s real strength:** Instant category comprehension for the old use case. But that use case is now a subset. Keeping it forces you to constantly explain “it’s actually more than stock”—wasting social proof.

**My opinion:** Rebrand now, pre-Product Hunt. A *renamed debut* is cleaner than a *rename-post-launch* announcement six months in. You lose zero existing trust because zero trust exists yet (20–50 friends). The cost of shifting is low; the cost of name regret grows with every user.

**Alternative worth a serious look:** *keep.id* (short, metaphor-friendly: “keep the identity,” multiple engines, works for walk cycles and stories, TLD is crisp). But if you want the poetic, differentiated, art-forward signal, **unstatic.art is worth the spelling friction.** Go.
