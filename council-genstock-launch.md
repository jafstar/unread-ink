# Council opinions: genstock.photo soft launch

## Claude

**Real talk: You're launching half a product. Here's what to do.**

**DON'T touch Product Hunt yet.** PH is a one-shot cannon – you get one launch, one burst of attention, and you're asking thousands of strangers to try something they can't pay for. You'll harvest emails and... then what? They'll forget you exist by the time billing works. PH is for *momentum*, not testing. Save it for 4-6 weeks when you can actually capture revenue.

**What this soft launch should actually be:**

A **private beta disguised as exclusivity**. Send 20-50 hand-picked invites (designers, indie game devs, children's book illustrators, webcomic artists) with a direct link and personal note. No public landing page yet. Make it feel like they're getting early access to something powerful, not joining a broken product.

**The real goal isn't validation – it's discovery.** You stumbled onto walk cycles and book illustration *tonight*. You don't know what you have yet. Use these 50 people to:
- Watch which use case they gravitate toward (stock photos vs. character consistency vs. something else)
- See where they get stuck or bored
- Find your actual positioning before you tell the world what you are

**Critical things NOT to do:**
- Don't call it a "stock photo tool" if character/illustration work is stronger – you'll attract the wrong users and miss your real market
- Don't apologize for missing billing. Just say "invite-only beta, free during this phase"
- Don't ask for feedback generally. Ask: "What did you try to make?" and "Where did it break?"

**One specific risk:** AI-generated faces have legal/ethical landmines. If someone uses your "identity lock" to clone a real person without consent, that's your liability surface. Write basic ToS *now* – before 50 people create 5,000 images.

**The win state:** In 3 weeks, you know whether you're a stock photo tool, an illustration platform, or a character design system. Then you launch the *right* product on Product Hunt with billing ready.

## Gemini

Okay, genstock.photo is live – fantastic. But no billing means this isn't a revenue launch; it's a **validation and refinement sprint.** Your key asset right now is that "identity-lock" differentiator, especially for book illustrations and walk cycles. That's your gold.

Here’s my take:

**The Soft Launch Goal (20-50 people):**
This launch is 100% about **deep, qualitative feedback** on your core mechanics and unique value proposition. You want to confirm the "wow" factor of identity-locking, identify showstopper bugs, and validate your UI/UX assumptions before you even *think* about pricing.

**Product Hunt Angle/Positioning (No Billing):**
**Be transparent and lean hard into "early access."** Your PH title should shout your unique differentiator, not just "AI stock photos."
*   **Title Idea:** "genstock.photo: Identity-Locked AI Generation for Coherent Characters & Illustrations (Early Access)"
*   **Tagline/Description:** "Upload a reference, lock its identity, then generate consistent characters across poses, scenes, and even 6-frame walk cycles. Free for early users – help us build the future of AI-powered creative consistency."
*   **Call to Action:** "We're in active development and looking for power users to stress-test our identity-lock tech. Your feedback directly shapes our roadmap."
*   **Benefit:** Frame the lack of billing as a **perk** for early adopters – unlimited free usage in exchange for their insights.

**What This Launch Should Actually Look Like:**
1.  **Direct Feedback Loop:** Implement a very obvious "Send Feedback" button in the app that links to a simple form (Google Forms, Typeform) or, better yet, a dedicated Discord channel where you and your team are actively present.
2.  **Guided Onboarding:** Create a quick 60-second video demo or a brief in-app tutorial highlighting the identity-lock mechanism. Don't assume users will immediately grasp its power.
3.  **Personal Engagement:** With 20-50 people, you can afford to personally reach out to your F&F group. Ask specific questions: "Did the identity hold? Was the tag system intuitive? Where did you get stuck?"
4.  **Heavy Monitoring:** Watch your error logs, compute usage, and user session recordings (if you have tools like Hotjar) like a hawk. Find out *why* people leave.

**What NOT to Do at This Scale:**
*   **DO NOT try to fake billing or imply it's coming "soon" without a firm date.** Transparency builds trust.
*   **DO NOT aim for virality or large user counts.** This is a surgical strike for feedback. More users now equals more noise and potentially more cost without the data you need.
*   **DO NOT treat all feedback equally.** Focus on validating the core "identity-lock" value proposition. Defer secondary feature requests until that's rock-solid.
*   **DO NOT run paid ads.** This is a waste of money without billing or a validated product-market fit.

**Specific Risks for a 20-50 Person Soft Launch:**
1.  **Compute Burnout:** AI generation is expensive. Even 50 active users generating variations across 3 engines can quickly eat through credits. Monitor your API costs constantly. You have no billing to offset this.
2.  **"Too Kind" Feedback:** Friends and family might not be critical enough. Product Hunt users will be more brutal and objective, which is good.
3.  **Discovery of Major Architectural Flaws:** A small group can still uncover fundamental stability issues or bottlenecks that will need immediate attention and could halt progress.
4.  **Reputational Damage (even small):** A bad experience, even for a few, can lead to negative sentiment spreading, especially if it hits a specific community (e.g., illustrators).

Good luck. Focus on learning, not earning, in this phase.

## ChatGPT

The launch of genstock.photo presents a unique opportunity to fine-tune and present a multifaceted AI tool that's not just about stock photos but also about progressive identity-locked generation. Given that billing isn't ready, this soft launch should emphasize community feedback and iterative development over commercial goals.

**Product Hunt Positioning:**

Highlight the innovative aspect: Focus on the novel "identity-lock" mechanism, emphasizing genstock.photo as an advanced artistic tool rather than purely a stock photo generator. Consider positioning it as a dual-purpose platform catering to both creative professionals seeking bespoke illustrations and traditional stock image users.

**Soft Launch Strategy:**

1. **Feedback-Driven Development:** Leverage the small community for detailed feedback. Highlight this in your Product Hunt campaign by presenting the launch as a collaborative evolution of a tool rather than a finished product. Encourage testers to share thoughts on the user experience, quality of outputs, and potential applications.

2. **Content and Case Studies:** Showcase sample outputs, especially the book-illustration-quality images and character walk cycles, as case studies on Product Hunt. This provides tangible evidence of the platform's unique capabilities and potential use cases.

3. **Clear Call to Action:** Without billing, frame the call to action as an invitation to become part of a growing community that shapes the future of AI-generated art. Offer exclusive access to future premium features for early adopters once billing is live.

**What NOT to Do:**

1. **Do Not Oversell:** Avoid framing the launch as a complete product. Misleading early adopters about full operational capabilities could damage trust and hinder future user acquisition.

2. **Do Not Ignore Feedback:** With a small user base, every piece of feedback is gold. Failure to actively engage and respond could discourage users and dampen community enthusiasm.

**Risks:**

- **Technical Glitches:** With new tech, expect some glitches. For this reason, ensure you have robust support in place to promptly address any issues that arise during usage.
  
- **Expectation Management:** Carefully manage expectations given the billing gap. While excitement is key, clarity about current limitations will help maintain user trust and engagement.

By focusing on the community aspect and the unique features of genstock.photo, you can build a solid foundation for success as you prepare for a broader launch.

## Radium

Here’s my real opinion, stripped of fluff.

**Your Product Hunt angle should NOT be “AI Stock Photo Generator.”** That positions you against Shutterstock, Midjourney, and every generic SDXL wrapper. Boring, and your billing gap makes monetization confusion deadly.

**Instead, lead with “identity-locked generation platform for character consistency”** – specifically the *walk cycle and book illustration* outputs you discovered tonight. That’s unique. PH audience loves “I can finally control character identity across poses/scenes.” Your description builder becomes a feature, not the pitch.

**What to actually DO for a 20-50 person soft launch:**

1. **Manual onboarding only.** No self-serve signup behind Stripe. Create a simple Google Form or Typeform: “Request early access.” You personally approve each person via email, send a one-time use link. This lets you iterate on the prompt system and billing setup *without* burning people on broken payment flows.

2. **Seed those 20-50 with a specific prompt.** Don’t say “generate whatever.” Say: “Generate a 6-frame walk cycle of a red-haired knight in armor, then try the same identity in a cyberpunk alley.” That exercises your differentiator. You’ll surface bugs in the identity-lock across engines.

3. **Demand feedback in exchange for access.** “Free for 2 weeks, for the cost of one 15-min call or a 3-sentence reply about what broke.” You need specific edge case data (e.g., “Flux identity lock broke when I uploaded a side-angle reference”). Generic “cool tool” feedback is useless.

**What NOT to do at this scale:**

- **Don’t optimize for virality.** No “share to unlock” or referrals. With no billing, you’ll attract tire-kickers who hit the free tier, generate 50 variants, vanish, and never return. That pollutes your retention metrics.

- **Don’t publicize the “stock photo” angle yet.** Public launch without payment = SEO spam, blog coverage calling you “free,” and expectations that can’t be walked back. Soft launch is your chance to set the *right* paid expectations.

- **Don’t give everyone all 3 engines.** Some users will max out expensive API calls on Gemini/Recraft. Cap at, say, 100 generations total across engines per account, or restrict to Flux-only with a “request access” note for the others unless they talk to you.

**Real risks specific to 20-50 people:**

- **Whale failure risk.** If one user generates 800 variations in 3 days on your dime, you’ll hit a $200+ API bill. Set a hard daily cap per user now.

- **False positive feedback risk.** Friends/family say “looks great!” because they like you. They won’t test the billing flow or the walk cycle edge case. You *must* recruit 3-5 strangers from a niche community (e.g., indie game devs needing sprite consistency) who have no emotional attachment to you.

- **Your biggest risk: the identity lock fails on edge cases (e.g., a non-front-facing reference, extreme poses) and you don’t discover it until PH launch.** Your 20-50 testers *must* explicitly try edge poses: profile, looking down, foreshortened, masked face. Give them that task list.

**TL;DR:** Position as “character consistency” on PH. No self-serve signup. Hard API caps. Recruit 5 strangers. Test edge case identity lock thoroughly. And for god’s sake, don’t let anyone call it a “free stock photo generator” in writing.
