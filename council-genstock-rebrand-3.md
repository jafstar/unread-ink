# Council opinions round 3: StoryGlue

## Claude

**StoryGlue: A decent metaphor hamstrung by two real problems.**

The "glue" metaphor actually works better than I expected. It captures the binding agent—the thing that holds a character's visual identity constant across different contexts. That's legitimately clever. But here's where it falls apart:

**Problem 1: "Story" is half the product, presented as the whole product.** You've got Photos (single subject consistency) and Story (narrative sequences). Naming the entire platform "StoryGlue" creates an immediate positioning problem. When someone wants to generate their headshot across 20 different professional settings, or a product mascot in various seasonal contexts, they're not working on a "story"—yet that's what your brand name promises. You're essentially naming a car company "HighwayDrive" when half your customers use it for city commuting. The name artificially narrows perceived use cases.

**Problem 2: The premium .com situation is a trap you should avoid.** StoryGlue isn't distinctive enough to justify aftermarket pricing. It's descriptive, it's two common words, and frankly, someone could launch "StoryBond" or "SceneGlue" tomorrow and create confusion. Paying premium dollars for a .com that isn't legally defensible or culturally sticky is poor capital allocation at your stage.

Between .ai and .app: **.app wins here**, but only because "glue" already softens the technical edge. StoryGlue.app reads as "tool for stories." StoryGlue.ai reads like AI trying too hard to sound creative.

**My actual recommendation: Ship keep.id immediately.**

Two of us independently converged on it for reasons that remain valid: it names the actual product truth (identity persistence), it's a single memorable syllable, .id is semantically perfect, and it doesn't artificially limit you to "stories" when half your product is about photos/subjects/characters in non-narrative contexts. 

StoryGlue is *fine*, but keep.id is *right*. You're at 20-50 friends pre-launch. This is exactly when you want a name that won't need explaining or apologizing for at scale. Don't overthink yourself into a narrower, more expensive, less defensible position.

**Ship keep.id.**

## Gemini

Alright, let's cut through the noise.

**My Opinion on StoryGlue:**

I'm rejecting "StoryGlue." It's a step backward from identifying your core differentiator.

1.  **"Glue" mechanism:** "Glue" fundamentally suggests assembly or sticking *disparate* things together. While you're "sticking" an identity across scenes, the dominant mental model of glue is additive, like collage or craft supplies. It doesn't convey the sophisticated "identity lock" of a single entity. It sounds more like a tool for piecing together static images rather than generating dynamic, consistent ones from a foundational identity. The meaning is too fuzzy for your key mechanic.

2.  **"Story" scope:** Naming your entire product "StoryGlue" when it offers both "Photos" (single subject, consistent identity) and "Story" (sequential scenes) is a mistake. It immediately narrows the product's perceived utility, alienating anyone who needs character consistency for a single image, an avatar, or a series of concept art where "story" isn't the primary goal. You're leading with an application, not your core technology.

3.  **.ai vs .app for StoryGlue:** If you *were* to go with StoryGlue, **storyglue.ai** would be the better choice. It signals the underlying AI tech, which is crucial for a generative product, and adds a modern, innovative sheen that "glue" itself doesn't provide. `.app` leans too much into cold utility for something that also has a creative output.

4.  **.com premium:** Absolutely **not worth it** at this stage. You have 20-50 friends using it pre-launch. Every dollar should go towards product development, user feedback, or actual growth. Paying a premium for a domain for a name that isn't even truly hitting your differentiator is throwing money away. .ai or .app are perfectly acceptable (and often preferred for AI products) for early-stage companies.

**Final Recommendation:**

Reject "StoryGlue" outright.

Your team already converged on **keep.id** as a top pick, and I agree. This name is concise, memorable, and directly speaks to your core differentiator: the *keeping* of a character's *identity* across varied generations. It highlights consistency without being rigid.

I strongly recommend you proceed with **keep.id**.
*   **Domain:** The fact that `keep.id` is available as a full domain name (using the .id TLD) is a huge bonus. It's unique, highly memorable, and directly relevant to your product's function. This is significantly stronger than `keepid.ai` or `keepid.app`.
*   **Clarity:** It clearly communicates what makes your product special: it *keeps* the *ID*.
*   **Scope:** It doesn't limit you to "stories" or "photos." It applies universally to any generation where identity consistency is key.

**Ship `keep.id` on the `.id` domain.** It's simple, powerful, and directly on brand.

## Radium

## My take on StoryGlue

**"Glue" is wrong for this product.**

My immediate association is arts-and-crafts: Elmer's, glue sticks, collage projects from elementary school. It suggests *assembling* pre-existing pieces—cutting out magazine images and pasting them together. That's not what you do. Your product *generates* from scratch with a locked identity; it doesn't splice or composite. "Glue" also implies visible seams, a patchwork quality—the exact opposite of what identity-lock promises (seamless consistency that *looks* natural, not stuck together). Furthermore, "glue" names the mechanism (adhesion), which council already rejected in Round 1 as the wrong framing. You need the *outcome* (coherent character across scenes), not the means.

**"Story" misrepresents half your product.**

You have two project types: Photos (single subject, no narrative arc) and Story (scene sequences). Leading with "Story" tells the Photos user they're in the wrong product. It also biases perception toward illustrated storytelling—books, comics, animations—when a huge use case is likely "consistent character for branding, avatars, product shots" (no story required). Unless you plan to sunset the Photos type entirely, this name forces a categorical dissonance every time someone opens the app wanting a single portrait.

**TLD recommendation: .ai, not .app.**

"StoryGlue.ai" reads as AI-native, creative, and expressive (the .ai TLD has built-in permission to feel experimental and visual). ".app" locks you back into utility-software coldness that council rightly flagged. And for this name specifically, .ai partially rescues the mismatch: "StoryGlue.ai" suggests *AI that helps glue stories together*, which is at least defensible. ".app" leaves you defenseless—it's just a craft supply.

**.com premium: skip.** $pre-launch with 20–50 friends, spending premium on a domain when the name itself has structural issues is burning capital. Even if StoryGlue were perfect, .ai works fine until you have traction. By the time you need .com, you'll either have budget or reason to rebrand again.

### Final answer: No, do not ship StoryGlue.

**Stick with `keep.id` from the earlier convergence.** It's the strongest candidate you've seen:

- **"Keep"** perfectly captures the identity-lock mechanism (you keep a character consistent across generations) and works for *both* Photos and Story modes—you keep someone's identity whether for one portrait or a whole sequence. It names the benefit, not the mechanic.
- **"Keep"** is a verb in command form (active, confident) and a noun (the thing you hold onto). Flexible.
- **`keep.id`** is short, memorable, and the `.id` TLD is perfect for "identity"—a meaningful, ownable suffix that reinforces the product's core promise. It doesn't muddy with "adhesive" or "narrative."
- It was your council's top pick *independently* across two rounds. They're telling you something.

**One alternative if you want a third option:** `continuum.com` (premium .com) or `continuum.ai`—it suggests unbroken identity across transformations, works for both project modes, feels elevated not crafty. But `keep.id` is more distinctive and already converged. Ship it.
