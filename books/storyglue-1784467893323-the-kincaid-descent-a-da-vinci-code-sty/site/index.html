The Breath of the Serpent

When disgraced field researcher Petra Keane—a geographer who maps places that shouldn’t exist, fired by the USGS for chasing a ghost canyon in Greenland—gets her hands on G.E. Kincaid’s actual journal, she realizes the underground city is not a myth but a real, hidden system keyed to the Grand Canyon’s specific rock formations. The journal’s symbols match the Temple of Isis, Tower of Ra, and Shiva Temple precisely, revealing a geological cipher pointing to ventilation shafts. Pursued by The Handlers, who seal tunnels and scrub evidence with surgical precision, Petra enlists a young Hopi guide, Tawa Lomahongva, whose grandmother warned him never to enter the canyon’s “true rivers.” As they descend, they discover silver tubes that pulse with light and ancient tablets that react to human biometric data, but Petra must confront that her father—a prominent archaeologist who vanished in the canyon twenty years ago—was the first to find the site and was silenced by the Smithsonian cover-up.
