# Plot pitches

Big answer: A thriller about bigfoot and bears, written for hikers

## Claude

**Working Title:** *The Ursus Protocol*

**Premise:** When a wilderness guide discovers that a reclusive wildlife biologist has been conducting illegal hybridization experiments in the North Cascades, she must navigate a deadly cat-and-mouse game through increasingly dangerous terrain while something massive and intelligent stalks both hunter and hunted through the forest.

**Protagonist:** Maya Okonkwo, 34, a former Park Service ranger turned freelance wilderness guide who left federal employment after a controversial incident involving a grizzly relocation. She's dealing with undiagnosed PTSD that manifests as hypervigilance in the backcountry—a trait that might now save her life. She runs ultra-marathons to manage anxiety and knows the Cascade backcountry better than most people know their neighborhoods.

**Point of View:** First-person present tense from Maya's perspective, which creates immediacy during pursuit sequences and allows readers to experience her growing paranoia and survival calculations in real-time.

**What Happens:** Maya is hired to guide Dr. AntonRessed, supposedly a visiting ecologist, into a remote research zone. On day three, she discovers his camp contains tranquilizer equipment and genetic material labeled with a defunct biotech company's logo. Before she can confront him, they encounter what appears to be a grizzly—until it stands fully upright at nearly eight feet and demonstrates problem-solving abilities no bear should possess. Dressed admits he's been tracking his "escaped subjects," bears enhanced with primate DNA intended for cognitive research, funded by a defense contractor interested in creating trainable biological weapons. The creatures are intelligent enough to set ambushes and hold grudges, and they've been systematically eliminating hikers who've come too close to their expanding territory, with deaths attributed to normal bear attacks. Maya realizes the missing hikers from the past two seasons weren't accidents. She must get evidence out to authorities while Dressed tries to silence her, and the creatures—there are three, possibly more—treat the pursuit as a hunt. The book climaxes when Maya uses her knowledge of bear behavior against the hybrids' expectations, turning their enhanced intelligence into overconfidence, and ultimately has to choose between exposing the truth and triggering a military sterilization of an entire wilderness area, or burying the secret to protect the land itself.

## Gemini

Apex Trail

A seasoned ultra-marathoner seeking solace on an isolated Pacific Northwest trail uncovers a horrifying convergence of natural predators and unnatural forces, as highly agitated bears and an ancient, territorial Bigfoot are revealed to be pawns in a shadowy scheme, trapping her in a desperate fight for survival where every step closer to the truth means venturing deeper into an ecological and human-engineered nightmare.

Dr. Eleanor "Ellie" Vance, a disillusioned environmental science professor and former Search and Rescue (SAR) lead, now a driven ultra-marathoner. She is fiercely independent, possesses encyclopedic knowledge of wilderness survival, animal behavior, and human psychology, but carries the weight of a past SAR failure that makes her wary of trust and pushes her to seek solitude in extreme environments.

Third-person limited, exclusively from Eleanor Vance's perspective.

Ellie Vance ventures deep into a rarely traversed section of the Olympic National Forest on a multi-day solo endurance run, seeking escape from professional disillusionment and personal grief. Her run quickly devolves into a nightmare when she observes unnatural aggression and coordinated pack behavior among grizzly bears, far beyond typical encounters. Her trail cameras capture fleeting, massive bipedal figures seemingly directing the bears, and she finds disturbing evidence of illegal clear-cutting. As Ellie attempts to report her findings, she’s met with stonewalling and thinly veiled threats, confirming her suspicion that something nefarious is at play. She soon discovers a ruthless, clandestine timber corporation is deliberately using specialized sound frequencies and pheromone dispersers to agitate both the bear population and a reclusive family of Sasquatch, driving them into violent conflict to create a "no-go" zone. This chaos serves as a smokescreen for their massive, illegal logging operation, designed to destroy a pristine ancient forest and extract valuable minerals. Ellie, a master of wilderness survival, must outwit not only the enraged bears and the territorial, now highly dangerous Sasquatch, but also the corporation's armed security detail, who are systematically hunting her to silence her. Her journey culminates in a desperate race against time to gather irrefutable evidence of the corporation's crimes, forcing her to forge a precarious, non-verbal alliance with the Sasquatch matriarch to lead the bears into a final, chaotic confrontation with the armed corporate goons, ensuring the destruction of the evidence and the forest itself is averted, even if it means her own escape is jeopardized.

## ChatGPT

Title: "Tracks in the Timber"

Premise: When a tech-weary woman sets out on a solo hiking trip through the remote forests of the Pacific Northwest, she stumbles upon a decades-old mystery involving a series of unsolved disappearances attributed to bigfoot and bears. As she uncovers secrets kept by a small mountain community, she soon realizes that the truth may be far more dangerous and elusive than the myths.

Protagonist: Sarah Whitmore, a burnt-out investigative journalist who has traded the chaos of city life for a quieter exploration of the natural world. She is determined, perceptive, and harbors an insatiable curiosity that once made her one of the most promising journalists of her generation.

Point of View: Third-person limited, focusing primarily on Sarah's experiences and perceptions.

Plot: Sarah embarks on a solo trek to reconnect with nature and escape the demands of her dormant career. However, she soon encounters locals in a nearby town who share chilling tales of hikers gone missing and suspicions of bigfoot sightings. Her journalistic instincts reignite when a new disappearance occurs, eerily mirroring those of the past. Sarah immerses herself in the investigation, piecing together clues that point to something more sinister than mythical creatures. As she delves deeper, the line between myth and menace blurs, leading her to a shocking conspiracy involving illegal bear poaching and deliberate efforts to maintain the local bigfoot legend. The climax builds in the forest's depths, where Sarah must confront not only the dangers of the wilderness but also those who manipulate its mystery for greed.

## Radium

**Working Title:** *The Boundary Shift*

**Premise:** Deep in the North Cascades, a series of grisly bear maulings are blamed on a rogue grizzly, but a backcountry ranger suspects the kills are too deliberate, too clean—something is moving between the bears and the treeline, and it’s learning to use them as weapons.

**Protagonist:** Nora Cruz, a 32-year-old backcountry ranger for the Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest. She’s a former wildlife biologist who left academia after her fieldwork on bear behavior was dismissed as “too anecdotal.” She’s stubborn, quiet, and reads the woods the way most people read a room.

**Point of View:** Close third-person, limited to Nora’s perspective—her tracking instincts, her sensory overload in the silent alpine, her growing dread that the woods are no longer at the top of the food chain.

**What Actually Happens:** The book opens with Nora finding a dead hiker halfway up the Copper Ridge trail, the body threaded up into a hemlock like a larder cache—but no bear tracks below. The Forest Service calls it a freak grizzly attack, but Nora notices the claw marks on the bark are too evenly spaced, almost tool-like. Over the next several weeks, she starts patrolling the high meadows at dusk and finding signs: a single massive footprint that stops at a talus field and doesn’t reappear, a bear den that has been dismantled from the outside in, the smell of something mossy and rank that hangs over the trail like a warning. She cross-references old Samsquatch reports from the 1970s with recent bear migration patterns and realizes the creature isn’t hiding from humans—it’s hiding *in* the bears, teaching them to maul on command, using their kills to mask its own existence. When a group of thru-hikers goes missing on the Pacific Crest Trail, Nora goes in alone, tracking the blood sign through a fog-shrouded basin. She finds the creature mid-ambush, working a black bear like a dog handler works a K-9, and she watches it kill the bear when the animal fails. The climax happens in a boulder field at night, where Nora uses her knowledge of bear defensive behavior to bait the creature into a landslide, burying it under tons of granite. But in the final scene, as she radios for extraction, she sees a second set of prints beside her own on the muddy trail—larger, fresher, coming from the opposite direction.
